üéØ The CLEAR Framework (Anthropic/OpenAI Best Practices)

  • Context: Provide relevant background and situational information
  • Length: Specify desired output length and detail level
  • Examples: Include 1-3 examples to demonstrate expected format
  • Audience: Define who will use this output and their expertise level
  • Role: Assign a specific expert role with credentials

üöÄ Advanced Techniques from AI Research

  • Chain of Thought: "Think step by step" improves reasoning by 40%+
  • Few-Shot Learning: 2-3 examples dramatically improve output quality
  • Role Playing: "You are a [specific expert]" increases accuracy
  • Task Decomposition: Break complex requests into numbered steps
  • Output Constraints: Set clear boundaries and quality criteria
  • Verification Prompts: Ask AI to double-check its own work

‚ùå Common Mistakes (Based on Research)

  • Vague Instructions: "Write something good" vs "Write a 500-word professional email"
  • Missing Context: AI needs background to provide relevant responses
  • No Examples: Show don't just tell - provide 1-2 example outputs
  • Weak Role Assignment: "Act as a writer" vs "You are a senior marketing copywriter with 10+ years experience"
  • No Quality Criteria: Specify accuracy, tone, and completeness requirements

üìù Research-Backed Template Structure

Optimal prompts follow this proven structure:

  1. Expert Role Assignment: "You are a [specific expert] with [credentials/experience]"
  2. Clear Task Definition: "Task: [specific action]"
  3. Context & Background: "Context: [relevant situation/information]"
  4. Detailed Instructions: Numbered list of specific requirements
  5. Output Format: Exact structure and style specifications
  6. Quality Criteria: "Ensure accuracy, clarity, and [specific standards]"
  7. Examples (Optional): 1-2 examples of desired output
